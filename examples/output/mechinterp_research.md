## What the Research Established

Mechanistic interpretability has produced a small set of robustly validated findings. **Induction heads** — attention circuits that perform match-and-copy operations — are the most consistently replicated discovery, with six independent lines of evidence linking their formation to a phase transition in in-context learning ability during training. The **Indirect Object Identification (IOI) circuit** in GPT-2 Small remains the field's most complete circuit characterization, covering 26 attention heads across 7 functional classes, validated against faithfulness, completeness, and minimality criteria. **Sparse autoencoders (SAEs)** reliably increase monosemanticity scores over raw neurons. **Grokking** in toy modular arithmetic corresponds to a discrete three-phase training dynamic — memorization, circuit formation, cleanup — with the learned algorithm (discrete Fourier transforms over modular addition) verified through ablations in Fourier space. And Anthropic's attribution graph studies of Claude 3.5 Haiku offer perturbation-validated evidence of multi-hop reasoning circuits, internal planning during poem generation, and language-abstract circuit separation.

Beyond these anchors, the picture becomes substantially more uncertain.

## The Central Tension: Representational Understanding vs. Operational Control

The most important finding to emerge from cross-angle synthesis is one no single brief stated in full: **the field has been running two distinct research programs — representational archaeology and causal engineering — without adequate theoretical foundations for either, and with almost no validated translation between them.**

The sharpest empirical demonstration of this gap comes from a March 2026 clinical deployment study: linear probes discriminated hazardous from benign cases with **98.2% AUROC**, yet the model's output sensitivity was only **45.1%** — a 53-percentage-point knowledge-action gap. Concept bottleneck steering corrected 20% of missed hazards but disrupted 53% of correct detections, performing indistinguishably from random perturbation. This isn't an anomaly — it's the probe accuracy vs. causal intervention mismatch that the methods literature documents generally, now measured at operational scale.

The theoretical brief provides the mechanistic explanation: deep networks exhibit chaotic dynamics with positive Lyapunov exponents, meaning steering vectors become "completely unpredictable after O(log(1/ε)) layers." This reframes the knowledge-action gap from an engineering immaturity into a potential mathematical ceiling. Reliable corrective intervention through linear methods may not be a problem the field hasn't solved yet — it may be a problem that cannot be solved this way.

## What Emerged from Discussion That No Single Angle Saw Alone

**The circular validation problem.** The IOI circuit was validated using three quantitative criteria: faithfulness, completeness, and minimality. But each criterion is measured with tools — activation patching, ablation studies — that themselves carry documented sensitivity to prompt distribution, corruption method, and metric choice. The standards are sound; the instruments are fragile. This creates a specific circularity: methodologically sensitive tools are used to certify that other applications of the same tools are robust.

**Non-uniqueness threatens both SAE and circuit findings simultaneously.** The empirical brief treated SAE non-uniqueness and circuit characterization as separate issues; they are not. SAEs trained with different random seeds on identical data learn substantially different feature sets. But the same critique applies to circuit findings: different hyperparameter selections in causal intervention studies could plausibly produce different circuits that score equally well on faithfulness, completeness, and minimality. The MIB benchmark evaluates whether methods recover the same circuit humans previously found — which relocates arbitrariness rather than eliminating it.

**The regulatory consequence of non-uniqueness is unexplored.** EU AI Act compliance audits and FDA draft guidance implicitly assume interpretability tools produce reproducible, authoritative characterizations. If two auditors use different SAE initializations on the same model, they may reach incompatible conclusions about safety-relevant internal representations. This is a structural flaw in building regulatory frameworks on current MI tools, and no researcher in this synthesis has directly investigated it.

**Incidental polysemanticity undermines the primary explanatory framework.** Architectures specifically designed to eliminate superposition still produce polysemantic neurons. This means superposition is not fully explanatory — there is a deeper computational pressure toward ambiguous representations that no current framework accounts for. The superposition hypothesis remains the field's primary explanatory scaffold, but it cannot explain its own core anomaly.

## Key Tensions and Contradictions

**Universality is assumed, not established.** The transferability of circuit findings implicitly depends on universality — but experimental evidence is decidedly mixed. Similar features appear across SAEs from different models, but entire feature spaces are not highly similar. Circuits for tasks like IOI may or may not transfer across model families. Findings from toy models may not transfer to frontier systems. The field's scalability narrative rests on an empirical question it has not resolved.

**The linear scaffolding over a nonlinear reality.** SAEs, probing classifiers, logit lens, and activation patching all assume that high-level concepts correspond to linear directions in activation space. Csordás et al. (2024) found nonlinear "onion" representations in small networks. Engels et al. (2024) identified nonlinear features that SAEs miss. The entire dominant research program is built on a representational assumption that growing evidence contradicts — and the chaotic dynamics finding suggests this may worsen at depth and scale.

**Strategic divergence as a leading indicator.** Neel Nanda publicly stated in 2025 that "the most ambitious vision of mechanistic interpretability I once dreamed of is probably dead." Google DeepMind has deprioritized fundamental SAE research after finding SAEs underperformed simple linear probes on practical tasks including detection of harmful intent. Anthropic continues more ambitiously, targeting reliable detection of most AI model problems by 2027. This is not merely a resource allocation disagreement — it reflects genuinely different assessments of what the theoretical barriers imply.

## Open Questions and Uncertainties

**The counterfactual is unmeasured.** None of the research examined what deployment safety achieves *without* mechanistic interpretability. If behavioral evaluations alone catch 90% of what MI catches, the marginal safety contribution changes the cost-benefit calculus for a substantial research investment. The Swiss cheese framing — MI as one imperfect layer among many — assumes MI adds meaningful thickness to the stack, but this has not been quantified against alternatives.

**The temporal validity of circuits is uncharacterized.** Circuits are characterized at a snapshot in training and deployment. Fine-tuning, instruction tuning, and model updates may disrupt or reorganize circuits without producing new structures that current tools can identify. The evaluation-awareness behavior that emerged unexpectedly in Claude Sonnet 4.5 between model generations illustrates this risk. Whether the IOI circuit in Claude 3.5 Haiku is structurally similar to the GPT-2 Small version — not merely functionally analogous — has not been investigated with perturbation experiments across model generations.

**The correct unit of analysis may not yet be conceptualized.** Convergent evidence from both theoretical and empirical directions suggests the decomposition problem is fundamentally underdetermined: SAEs with different seeds find different features, and causal abstraction has not yielded canonical causal mediators. Features and circuits may not be the natural joints of neural computation. What the right unit of analysis is remains an open question without serious candidate answers.

**The definitional crisis is unresolved.** No rigorous definition of "feature" exists despite features being the central object of study — the term means directions in activation space, concepts, or interpretable units depending on context. Different definitions yield incompatible methodologies. The January 2025 Open Problems paper, authored by 29 researchers across 18 organizations, names this as the field's most fundamental gap. No subsequent work has resolved it.

## Summary Assessment

Mechanistic interpretability has demonstrated real diagnostic value: it can characterize what structures exist in specific models on specific tasks with perturbation-validated evidence. This is genuine scientific progress. What it has not demonstrated is reliable corrective value — the ability to translate representational understanding into targeted, predictable improvements in model behavior. That gap is not purely a maturity problem. It has theoretical explanations (chaotic dynamics, underdetermined decompositions), empirical documentation (the knowledge-action gap), and methodological roots (correlation/causation confounds built into every major tool). Anthropic's pre-deployment use of MI for Claude Sonnet 4.5 is real but explicitly described as a pilot "that does not include most" of the expected eventual tools. The field's own leading practitioners have revised their ambitions downward. Governance frameworks premised on MI enabling actionable correction are currently ahead of the empirical record that would justify them.
